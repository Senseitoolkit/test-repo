Explore & Learn 📚

Tap a card to open it. These tips help you become a debate star!

A debate is a friendly talk where two teams have different ideas about the same question. Each team tries to explain why their idea is best, using good reasons.

It's like a game of ideas! Nobody has to be mean — you win by thinking clearly and speaking well. 🌟

  • 🎤 Speakers — the team members who share ideas.
  • 🧑‍🏫 Moderator — usually the teacher! They keep time and make sure everyone takes turns.
  • 👏 Audience — the rest of the class. They listen carefully and vote at the end.

Use the P-B-E trick to build a strong point:

P — Point
Say what you think.
"I think recess should be longer."
B — Because
Give your reason.
"Because kids focus better after a break."
E — Example
Show proof or a story.
"For example, after playing I'm ready to read!"
"I believe that…"
"I think… because…"
"My team agrees that…"
"I disagree because…"
"That's a good idea, but…"
"For example, …"
"Another reason is…"
"In conclusion, …"
  • 👂 Listen when the other team is speaking.
  • 🙋 Take turns — wait for your turn to talk.
  • 🧠 Argue the idea, not the person. No put-downs!
  • 🤝 Be a good sport — say "good debate!" at the end.
Opinion: What you think or feel.
Fact: Something true that can be checked.
Reason: The "because" that backs up your idea.
Evidence: Proof, like an example or a number.
Argument: Your idea plus your reasons (not a fight!).
Rebuttal: When you answer the other team's idea.
For / Against: The two sides of the question.
Conclusion: Your big finish — your last words.
