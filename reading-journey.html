How this guide fits together

Word reading follows Jolly Phonics, completed across Reception, and the complete Jolly Grammar programme, covered in Year 1. From Year 2 onwards, spelling and grammar carry forward through the National Curriculum English programmes of study, while the focus of reading shifts to fluency, vocabulary and comprehension. Comprehension is taught and assessed through the VIPERS strands — Vocabulary, Inference, Prediction, Explanation, Recall and Sequence/Summarise — created by Rob Smith (The Literacy Shed), which mirror the 2014 National Curriculum reading content domains: 1a–1e at KS1 and 2a–2h at KS2.

Standardised assessment using NGRT and Pearson GL begins in Year 2 and is tracked each term. These tests report a standard age score (national average 100) and a stanine from 1 (low) to 9 (high). A stanine of 7 sits in the above-average band — the baseline target in this framework, applied from Year 2 and sustained through to Year 6. The two threads running through every stage — noticing text features, and reading beyond the text — turn confident decoders into thoughtful, critical readers.
